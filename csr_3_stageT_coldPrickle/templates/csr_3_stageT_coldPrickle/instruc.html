You are about to start Part 3 of the study.

In this part, you will first be assigned to be either a Role A participant or a Role F participant. You will be informed of your role through the computer screen and you will keep the same role throughout the rest of the study.

Every Role A participant will be paired with a Role F participant. You will have the same partner throughout the rest of the study.

You will be told how much your partner contributed (overall on average) to the Group Exchange in Part 2.

The Role A participant will make the first decision. He or she will choose between A1 and A2.

  • A1 means the round ends and both participants receive the amount they would have earned if they contributed to the group account equal to the average amount they contributed in Part 2.

  • A2 means Role F will be asked to increase their contribution over and above what their average contribution to the Group Exchange in Part 2.

If the Role A participant chooses A2, then the Role F participant makes the next decision:

  • F1 means Role F increases their contribution to the group exchange in Part 2 by 1.5 times their average contribution. Role A will contribute to the group exchange their average contribution from Part 2.

  • F2 means Role F maintains their original contribution to the group exchange. The Role A participant will then be asked to make another decision.

If the Role F participant chooses F2, the Role A participant chooses between A3 and A4:

  • If the Role A participant chooses A3, both participants again receive the amount they earned during Part 2.

    • N2: With probability 0.25, the Role F participant will contribute the same amount that he or she originally contributed.

    • In N1 and N2 Role A will contribute twice as much to the Group Exchange.
